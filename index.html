About This Project

This website was created to combine two interests: coffee brewing and data engineering.

Rather than relying only on memory, each brew is documented, measured, and analyzed to identify the factors that influence coffee quality and consistency.

Data Engineering Barista is a coffee experiment journal that combines measurement, observation, and testing to improve each brew.

Every coffee preparation is treated like an experiment. Variables are documented, results are recorded, and future tests are planned to better understand how equipment and brewing techniques affect the final cup.

Each brew is treated like a mini data pipeline. Inputs are collected, transformed through the brewing process, stored in the brew log, analyzed, and used to improve the next cup.

Current Experiment

Brew #001 tested an E&B Lab by IMS Competition Moka Filter together with an AeroPress paper filter in a Bialetti 3-Cup Moka Pot.

Initial observations showed a cleaner and smoother cup compared to previous brews.

The next experiment will use the E&B filter without a paper filter to compare clarity, body, sediment, and overall taste.
